Matthew Du is currently a second-year Mechanical Engineering student studying at the University of Toronto, with self-initiated minors in Computer Science (the one offered by the Faculty of Arts and Science) and Robotics and Mechatronics. Matthew is interested in a wide various different topics and disciplines across Mechanical Engineering and Computer Science, such as:

  • Manufacturing of Mechatronics Systems
  • Computer Vision/Geometry Processing and Machine Learning
  • Robotics Control & Programming
  • Computational & Theoretical Fluid Mechanics
  • Interactive Robotics & Human Robot Interaction
  • Algorithm Design

Matthew aspires to become a robotics engineer in the future, working on personal, wearable, and assistive robotics and electronics to be able to help people. Throughout all his experiences working in Robotics, whether that would be in a school clubs or in a research lab, Matthew had always noticed that despite the amount of work and usefulness of the robots that he was building, people are not using them and robots are not being integrated into society. He wants to change that and build robots that people can actually use to make their lives better rather than hearing about how others are using them, whether that would be automating unpleasant or repetitive tasks or accommodating for some disability.

Some soft skills that Matthew possesses is excellent creativity and innovation, as well as strong leadership and ethical judgement. He is reliable, assertive, and helpful on teams, although he is currently working on how to communicate better and delegate tasks more equally with his team.

Outside of his curricular activities, Matthew is an avid musical performer and arranger/composer with various musical groups in the GTA. His primary instrument is Violin, but he is also proficient in various other instruments, such as Viola, Clarinet, Bass Clarinet, Saxophone and many more.
