About Me

Notable Projects

Mechanical Engineering

3D Printed Gearbox

This is a design and 3D printing for a gearbox. The design utilizes a 3:1 gear reduction ratio, where the input and output shafts are offset by 90 degrees and are not collinear. The design features 2 spur gears to achieve the gear ratio, and two bevel gears at a ratio of 1:1 to achieve the 90 degrees offset.

This is the summer research project that I was involved with in the summer of 2022 at the Microfluidics and BioMEMS Lab (MBL) at the University of Toronto in collaboration with Zhanfeng Zhou and under the supervision of Dr Xinyu Liu. Video Recording App

Source

This is a relatively small video recording app I designed using OpenCV and PyTorch. I trained a custom YOLOv5 model for hand tracking, which allows me to code in objects to follow hand gestures. Additionally, I used a pretrained RESNET for background filtering to change a virtual background. Due to the slow speed of these models, it is multithreaded to reduce the latency in the program.

Timetable Management App

Source

This is a program that was created as a part of the design project in the Fall 2021 term of CSC207H1 at the University of Toronto St.George. It is a timetable planning tool, that gathers information from the (now deprecated) U of T Course Finder using the JSoup library.

Some features include:

  • Students can search and add course or other tasks to their timetable, which is displayed on the GUI developed using Java Swing.
  • Students can load and unload to a serialized file with their courses using a custom file extension
  • Students can automatically schedule multiple courses into their timetable using a Depth First Search Algorithm
