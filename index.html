Matthew Du

I am currently a second-year Mechanical Engineering student studying at the University of Toronto with way too many minors, including a minor in Computer Science (the one offered by the Faculty of Arts and Science at the University of Toronto).

I am interested in a wide variety of subjects, with particular focus in,

  • Robotics and Mechatronics
  • Machine Learning
  • Computer Vision & graphics
  • Assistive and Personal Devices

Particularly, I enjoy working on projects that span across multiple disciplines, whether that would be a personal device combining elements of computer vision and mechatronics, or the study of human factors in human robot interaction

I am also the co-president and co-founder of the newly revived Student Branch of the Canadian Society of Mechanical Engineers (CSME) at the University of Toronto. It is a professional development organization designed to help bridge the gap between academia and industry for mechanical engineering students at the University of Toronto. Since it is relatively new, we are still setting things up and building our network. If you are an organization that would like to partner with us in hosting events to gain access to talented mechanical engineering students at the University of Toronto, feel free to email me at matthew.du@mail.utoronto.ca.

Outside school, I am an avid musical performer and arranger/composer with various musical groups in the Greater Toronto Area. My primary instrument is the Violin, but I am also proficient in numerous other instruments, such as Viola, Clarinet, Bass Clarinet, and Saxophone, to name a few.
