I am currently a second-year Mechanical Engineering student studying at the University of Toronto, with self-initiated minors in Computer Science (the one offered by the Faculty of Arts and Science) and Robotics and Mechatronics.

I am interested in a wide variety of topics and disciplines across Engineering and Computer Science, such as:

  • Design and Fabrication of Mechatronics Systems
  • Robotics Control & Programming
  • Computational & Theoretical Fluid Mechanics
  • Computer Vision & Geometry Processing
  • Interactive Robotics & Human Robot Interaction
  • Machine Learning
  • Algorithm Design

I aspire to become a robotics engineer in the future, working on personal, wearable, and assistive robotics and electronics to help people by automating repetitive tasks. While working in research labs and school clubs, I have noticed that, despite the labour involved and the overall utility of robots, people are not using them and are not integrated into society. I want to change that and build robots that people can use to enrich their lives, not just hear about how others use them on the news.

Some soft skills that I possess are excellent creativity and innovation, as well as strong leadership and ethical judgment. I am known to be reliable, assertive, and highly organized in teams, but currently working on how to work better and delegate tasks more equally.

Outside school, I am an avid musical performer and arranger/composer with various musical groups in the Greater Toronto Area. My primary instrument is the Violin, but I am also proficient in numerous other instruments, such as Viola, Clarinet, Bass Clarinet, and Saxophone, to name a few.
